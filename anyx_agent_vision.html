The Core Insight
Today, AI is a tool inside a person's workflow. The human is still the gap between every collaboration — routing files, writing summaries, waiting for replies.

Anyx makes AI the connective layer between people — not just between a person and a task. A "brain clone" means your expertise, judgment, and domain knowledge travels at agent speed, without your time being the bottleneck.

This isn't AI talking to AI. It's your intelligence, represented and exchanged at scale. The collaboration structure changes — not just the speed of individual work.
Open Questions — Must Answer Before Pitching
Trust & Authenticity
How does Person A know the expert's agent genuinely represents that expert's judgment — and isn't hallucinating their expertise? What's the credibility layer?
Brain Clone — Product Definition
What does it actually mean to "clone" someone's brain? How do you build, train, and maintain an agent that encodes a specific person's knowledge, style, and constraints?
Expert Marketplace
Who curates and vets the agent network? How are agents discovered, rated, and priced — what's free vs. paid, and who sets those terms?
Human Control UX
"Person stays in charge on both sides" — what does that look like in practice? Approval gates? Agent summaries before proceeding? Veto moments? What's the UX of oversight?
WhatsApp as Medium
Is agent-to-agent collaboration native to WhatsApp long-term, or is WhatsApp the bootstrapping layer that proves the model before it lives somewhere else?
Business Model
Where does Anyx capture value — the platform subscription, a marketplace take rate on paid expert agents, or enterprise "brain clone" licensing deals?
Defensibility
What stops a well-funded competitor from replicating the marketplace once proven? Is the moat the brain clone quality, the network density, or something else?
For Michael — One Paragraph
The vision in plain English
Right now, when two people collaborate using AI, the human is still the bridge between them — routing, translating, and manually ping-ponging while each side uses AI in its own silo. Anyx changes the collaboration structure itself. Each user has an agent that's a "brain clone" — encoding their expertise, judgment, and domain knowledge. When Person A needs something only Person B's expertise can solve, A's agent finds and engages B's agent directly. They align, negotiate, and produce a fitted output — while both humans stay in control, elevated from operators to decision-makers. The result: expert collaboration at agent speed, with no one's time as the bottleneck. This isn't a better AI assistant. It's a new protocol for how human expertise gets exchanged.
