Core insight

10 high-profile early users all independently say Anyx changed how they work. The wow moment takes time to reach — it requires either a curious self-starter or the founders manually guiding the user. Currently, the founders are the onboarding layer. This doesn't scale. Everything in this plan flows from solving that one problem.

Onboarding paths
Self-driven path
Orchestrator & Independent
  • Explores alone until the wow moment hits
  • Higher friction, higher ownership once they get it
  • Needs clear capability signals early
  • Needs one killer first use case to latch onto
Guided path
Operator & Dealmaker
  • Needs founders in the chat to unlock the wow
  • Fastest path to activation, doesn't scale yet
  • Needs a concrete "try this now" moment
  • Needs a real result within the first session

Interview guide

18 questions · 5 phases · 30–40 min · Don't read verbatim — use as anchors

Before booking formal interviews: Send an informal text to all 10 today: "Hey, I'd love 30 minutes to hear more about how you're using Anyx. When works this week?" No pitch. No program mention. Just book the calls.
Phase 1 — Warm up & context 5 min · 3 questions
01
Walk me through a typical day at work before Anyx — what did your morning look like?
Sets the before-state baseline. Get them feeling the pain before discussing the solution.
All archetypes
02
What were you using before Anyx to manage your work — tools, habits, workarounds?
Surfaces competitive landscape and reveals how sophisticated they are.
All archetypes
03
What was the specific frustration that made you willing to try something new?
The "job to be done" question. This becomes your GTM pain point language.
OperatorDealmakerIndependent
Phase 2 — The friction zone 8 min · 4 questions
04
When you first started using Anyx, what confused you or felt unclear?
Opens the door to honest friction without making it feel like a complaint.
All archetypes
05
Was there a moment where you almost gave up or lost confidence it would work for you?
The dropout risk question. This is where you find the exact moment your onboarding needs to intervene.
OperatorIndependent
06
How long did it take before you felt like you actually understood what Anyx could do?
Quantifies the time-to-competence gap. If it's days or weeks, that's your onboarding problem statement.
All archetypes
07
Did someone help you figure it out, or did you work through it alone?
Directly maps to guided vs self-driven paths. If guided — who, what did they do, what unlocked it?
OrchestratorDealmaker
Phase 3 — The wow moment 10 min · 5 questions
08
Tell me about the moment it clicked. What were you doing, what happened, and how did it feel?
The core question. Let them tell it as a story. Don't interrupt. Write down their exact words.
All archetypes
09
What specific feature or action triggered that feeling?
Forces specificity. Was it inbox triage? A Groopy summary? First automated action? This becomes the activation target.
All archetypes
10
What did you do right after that moment — did you tell anyone, try something else, or just keep going?
The organic virality signal. If they told someone immediately, that's your referral trigger.
OperatorOrchestrator
11
If you had to describe that moment to a skeptic in one sentence, what would you say?
⭐ This is your marketing copy. Write it verbatim. The best product descriptions come from users, not founders.
All archetypes
12
Looking back, was there anything that could have gotten you to that moment faster?
Retrospective onboarding design input. They'll often name exactly the thing you need to build.
DealmakerIndependent
Phase 4 — Current usage & value 8 min · 3 questions
13
Walk me through how you use Anyx in a typical day now. What does it actually do for you?
Maps current usage patterns by archetype. Tells you where the product is sticky.
All archetypes
14
What would break in your work if Anyx disappeared tomorrow?
The dependency test. Your retention and value proof point — use in sales and case studies.
OperatorOrchestratorIndependent
15
Is there something you keep wishing Anyx could do that it can't yet?
Product roadmap input. Patterns across archetypes = feature priority.
All archetypes
Phase 5 — Advocacy & referral 7 min · 3 questions
16
Have you told anyone about Anyx? What did you say, and what was their reaction?
Maps organic referral behavior. Who did they tell? What words did they use? Did the other person sign up?
All archetypes
17
Who in your network would get the most value from this — and what would make you comfortable introducing them?
The warm intro unlock question. You're not asking them to refer — you're asking what would make it easy.
OperatorDealmaker
18
If I gave you a card that said "Anyx changed how I work" — what would you write on the other side to convince a peer to try it?
⭐ Your testimonial, champion quote, and referral message all in one. Write it verbatim.
